DESIGN DIRECTION: MAXIMUM PERCEIVED VALUE

This page must feel more expensive than a normal legal intake page.

The visual goal is:
premium accident triage with serious infrastructure underneath

NOT:
generic legal lead generation
NOT:
a white SaaS wrapper
NOT:
a billboard-lawyer funnel
NOT:
a hospital-looking intake page

BACKGROUND WRAPPER DIRECTION
Do NOT use a pure white full-page wrapper.
Do NOT keep the entire page black from top to bottom either.

Use this cadence:
- dark premium hero
- warm ivory / soft stone wrapper through the middle of the page
- white or soft-white only inside elevated cards, answer chips, form inputs, FAQ panels, and small supporting surfaces
- dark premium final CTA section
- dark premium confirmation screen

This creates:
- emotional relief for injured users
- stronger readability
- more visual cadence
- higher perceived value
- better alignment with the homepage brand family

COLOR SYSTEM
Primary shell:
deep navy-charcoal radial gradient

Light wrapper:
warm ivory / soft stone / light sand
NOT bright white

Accent:
subtle warm gold / champagne / soft bronze
used sparingly for progress, highlights, dividers, active states

Card surfaces:
soft white or off-white
with elegant borders and soft depth

TEXTURE / MATERIAL FEEL
The page should feel layered and tactile.
Use:
- glassmorphism only where tasteful
- soft shadows
- edge lighting
- subtle glow
- premium blur surfaces
- large rounded containers
- clean dividers
- no flat template feel

SECTION CADENCE
Alternate section energy with intention:
- Hero: dark, immersive, premium
- Emergency strip: lighter but crisp and high-contrast
- How CasePort Helps: warm light wrapper
- Accident types: warm light wrapper with elevated premium cards
- What to Do After an Accident: editorial premium light section
- Secure Case Check preview: darker inset panel inside lighter wrapper so it feels productized
- Documents section: warm light wrapper
- Trust and reassurance: lighter premium architecture
- FAQ: soft elevated accordion panels
- Final CTA: dark again
- Confirmation screen: dark again

This is important:
the injured page should breathe more than the law-firm page.
The law-firm side can stay more command-center.
The injured side should still feel premium, but safer, softer, and easier.

HERO DIRECTION
The hero should be one of the highest perceived-value sections on the site.

Requirements:
- dark premium shell
- strong CasePort wordmark
- calm sticky phone CTA
- refined right-side secure intake visual
- no fake dashboards
- no cheesy icons
- no stock-photo lawyer visuals

Hero should feel like:
“guided secure intake is already in motion”

Use:
- elegant layered form preview
- one sample step visible
- premium chips / progress / answer cards
- subtle activity cue
- soft glow and glass depth
- strong whitespace
- very controlled motion

PHONE CTA TREATMENT
Keep the sticky phone functionality because it helps trust.

But make it feel expensive:
- label: Call Now
- optional support line: Questions? Speak with us
- no loud vanity-number billboard feel
- no cheap floating button look
- make it feel like concierge support, not sales pressure

FORM PREVIEW SECTION
This must be one of the standout sections.

Do:
- make it feel like a real product
- elegant partially filled progress bar
- visible phases:
  Accident
  Treatment
  Fit
  Documents
  Contact
- show one real question with answer chips
- make completion feel easy and safe

Do NOT:
- show “0%”
- show placeholder blocks
- make it feel like a wireframe

TRUST / REASSURANCE ARCHITECTURE
Trust should feel integrated, not pasted on.

Use:
- calm reassurance cards
- premium spacing
- subtle iconography
- restrained hierarchy
- no fake badges
- no loud compliance stickers
- no gimmicky security chest-thumping

The trust feeling should come from:
- clarity
- restraint
- polish
- structure
- thoughtful microcopy

TYPOGRAPHY
- strong headline hierarchy
- bold but elegant
- generous line spacing
- body copy should feel breathable
- no cramped sections
- no narrow columns that feel boxed in
- every paragraph should scan easily under stress

MOTION
Use restrained motion only.
- smooth section transitions
- subtle hover states
- premium progress movement
- gentle pulse only where it helps
- no flashy animation
- no busy effects

INJURED-PERSON LENS
Put yourself in the shoes of someone hurt, stressed, and uncertain.

The design should reduce:
- overwhelm
- fear
- friction
- visual noise

The design should increase:
- calm
- trust
- clarity
- sense of progress
- willingness to complete the form

CONFIRMATION SCREEN DIRECTION
The confirmation screen should feel like active triage is in motion.

Use:
- dark premium background
- elegant countdown timer
- subtle pulse / processing state
- calm reassurance
- clear next-step checklist
- emotionally reassuring tone

It should NOT feel like:
a dead thank-you page

FINAL QUALITY BAR
When finished, the injured page should feel:
- calmer than generic injury funnels
- more premium than most legal websites
- easier to complete than typical intake flows
- clearly part of the CasePort brand family
- visually richer and more expensive than a plain black or plain white page
- built to maximize perceived value at every touchpoint
