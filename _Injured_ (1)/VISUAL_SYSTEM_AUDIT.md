# CasePort Visual System Audit — Reference Page Analysis

## **Overall Aesthetic**
- **Dark, sophisticated, tech-forward** (not light/white like current injured page)
- **Deep navy/black background** (#0A0E17 or similar)
- **Neon green (#00FF00) and cyan (#22D3EE) accents** — very prominent
- **Neon red (#FF0000) for warnings/alerts** — used sparingly
- **Dashed borders** — signature design element throughout
- **Grid-based layout** with intentional asymmetry
- **Minimal white space** — dark, immersive feeling
- **Tech/data-forward aesthetic** — not legal, not corporate, but infrastructure

## **Key Visual Elements**

### **1. Neon Green (#00FF00)**
- Used for: Section labels (UPPERCASE), badges, accent highlights, dashed borders
- Psychology: Energy, urgency, "live" data
- Prominence: Very high — draws attention immediately

### **2. Cyan (#22D3EE)**
- Used for: Primary CTAs, headings, interactive elements
- Psychology: Trust, tech, innovation
- Prominence: High — primary color

### **3. Neon Red (#FF0000)**
- Used for: "LIMITED" badges, warnings, status indicators
- Psychology: Scarcity, urgency, attention
- Prominence: Medium — used strategically

### **4. Dark Navy/Black (#0A0E17)**
- Background color
- Creates immersive, premium feel
- Contrast with neon colors is striking

### **5. Dashed Borders**
- Signature CasePort design element
- Used on cards, sections, CTAs
- Creates visual rhythm and sophistication

## **Typography**
- **Display font**: Bold, geometric, modern (likely Geist or similar)
- **Body font**: Clean, readable (likely Geist Sans)
- **Uppercase labels**: Heavy use of UPPERCASE for section labels
- **Letter-spacing**: Generous, creates breathing room
- **Font weights**: Bold for emphasis, light for secondary

## **Layout Patterns**

### **Hero Section**
- Asymmetric layout (text left, visual right)
- Large headline with cyan accent
- Subheadline with neon green highlight
- CTA button with dashed border

### **Card/Grid Sections**
- Dark cards with dashed cyan borders
- Neon green accents for labels
- Hover effects (likely lift/glow)
- Consistent spacing

### **Market Status Indicators**
- Color-coded badges: "LIMITED" (red), "OPEN" (cyan), "CAPPED" (gray), "IN REVIEW" (yellow)
- Shows real-time data
- Creates sense of urgency and live infrastructure

### **Testimonials**
- Dark cards with dashed borders
- Quote marks or styling
- Author name + company
- Neon accent for emphasis

## **Interactive Elements**

### **Buttons**
- Cyan background with dashed border
- Hover: Glow effect, lift animation
- Text: White/light with uppercase
- CTA text is specific and action-oriented

### **Navigation**
- Sticky header
- Neon green highlights on active/hover
- Minimal, clean design

## **Visual Hierarchy**
1. **Hero headline** — Largest, cyan + neon green
2. **Section labels** — Uppercase, neon green, small
3. **Subheadings** — Large, white
4. **Body text** — Medium, light gray
5. **Micro-copy** — Small, muted gray

## **Data Visualization**
- **Market cards** — Show: City, State, MII Score, Cases/Year, Partner Count
- **Color-coded status** — RED (limited), CYAN (open), GRAY (capped), YELLOW (in review)
- **Real-time indicators** — Dot animations, live updates
- **Heatmap-style calendar** — Shows activity/engagement

## **What the Reference Page Does Well**
✅ Dark, immersive, premium feel
✅ Neon colors create urgency and energy
✅ Dashed borders are signature design element
✅ Data-forward aesthetic (infrastructure, not legal)
✅ Color-coded status system is intuitive
✅ Asymmetric layouts avoid generic feel
✅ Uppercase labels create hierarchy
✅ Hover effects are smooth and purposeful

## **What the Current Injured Page Is Missing**
❌ **Dark background** — Currently light/white (wrong for brand)
❌ **Neon red status indicators** — Not using red for urgency
❌ **Data-forward aesthetic** — Too legal, not infrastructure
❌ **Consistent dashed border treatment** — Some elements missing
❌ **Uppercase section labels** — Should be more prominent
❌ **Color-coded status system** — Should show market availability
❌ **Heatmap/calendar element** — Could show activity
❌ **Premium dark immersion** — Feels too light and corporate

## **Recommendations for Injured Page**

### **1. Switch to Dark Background**
- Change from light to dark navy (#0A0E17)
- Adjust text colors for contrast
- Keep white/light text for readability

### **2. Amplify Neon Colors**
- More neon green for urgency signals
- More neon red for scarcity/limited availability
- Cyan for primary CTAs and trust signals

### **3. Add Data-Forward Elements**
- Show "Live Market Status" for claimant's area
- Color-coded availability: "2 of 3 firms available in Los Angeles"
- Real-time metrics dashboard (cases matched, avg settlement, response time)

### **4. Use Uppercase Labels Consistently**
- "CASE ACQUISITION SYSTEM"
- "REAL-TIME PROOF"
- "VERIFIED STANDARDS"
- "LIMITED AVAILABILITY"

### **5. Strengthen Dashed Border Treatment**
- Apply to all card elements
- Use as visual rhythm element
- Consistent thickness and color

### **6. Add Status Indicator System**
- RED: "LIMITED" (scarcity)
- CYAN: "AVAILABLE" (open)
- GREEN: "QUALIFIED" (success)
- YELLOW: "IN REVIEW" (processing)

### **7. Add Market Availability Widget**
- Show "2 of 3 firms available in [City]"
- Real-time updates
- Color-coded status
- Creates urgency and localization

### **8. Add Activity Heatmap**
- Show "847 claimants connected this month"
- Calendar-style visualization
- Shows momentum and social proof

## **Visual Tone Shift**
**Current**: Corporate, legal, light, safe
**Target**: Premium, tech-forward, dark, urgent, data-driven

The injured page should feel like you're accessing an exclusive infrastructure platform, not just filling out a legal form.
