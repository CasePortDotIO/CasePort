## **TRADE SECRET #1: The "Statute of Limitations" Arbitrage**

### **What Nobody Talks About**

Most lead aggregators treat all auto accident claims the same. They don't.

**The Hidden Truth**: The value of a lead is directly tied to how much time the claimant has left on the statute of limitations.

### **How It Works**

**Statute of Limitations for Auto Accidents** (varies by state):
- California: 2 years
- Texas: 2 years
- Florida: 4 years
- New York: 3 years
- Illinois: 2 years

**Lead Value Timeline**:
- Accident happened 1 month ago: Lead worth $1,500 (22 months left)
- Accident happened 6 months ago: Lead worth $1,200 (18 months left)
- Accident happened 12 months ago: Lead worth $900 (12 months left)
- Accident happened 18 months ago: Lead worth $500 (6 months left)
- Accident happened 22 months ago: Lead worth $100 (2 months left, URGENT)

**Why**: Firms have more time to work the case if the accident is recent. Old cases are riskier (more likely to expire).

### **The Trade Secret**

**Track the accident date obsessively.**

When a claimant calls, ask: "When did the accident happen?"

Then:
1. Calculate days remaining on statute of limitations
2. Price the lead accordingly
3. Prioritize leads with less time remaining (sell first)
4. Hold leads with more time remaining (sell later)

**Example Pricing Strategy**:
```
Days Remaining | Price/Lead | Urgency
730+ days      | $800       | Low
365-730 days   | $1,200     | Medium
180-365 days   | $1,500     | High
90-180 days    | $2,000     | Very High
0-90 days      | $3,000     | CRITICAL
```

**Why Firms Pay More**: Firms are desperate to close cases before statute expires. They'll pay 3-4x more for leads with 30 days left.

**Your Competitive Advantage**: Most aggregators don't track this. You do. You can charge 2-3x more for time-sensitive leads.

**Why**: Firms only want high-value cases. They won't take a case with $15K policy limit (not worth their time).

**Get the insurance policy limit from the claimant.**

Ask: "Do you know the at-fault driver's insurance policy limit?"

1. Ask: "What kind of car did they drive?" (luxury cars = higher limits)
2. Ask: "Did they have commercial insurance?" (commercial = higher limits)
3. Ask: "Was it a company vehicle?" (company = higher limits)

Then:
1. Filter out low-limit cases ($15K-$50K)
2. Prioritize high-limit cases ($250K+)
3. Price accordingly

**Example Pricing Strategy**:

```
$100K-$250K | $1,200     | High
$250K-$500K | $1,800     | Very High
$500K+      | $2,500     | PREMIUM
```

**Why Firms Pay More**: High policy limits = higher settlement potential = more profitable cases.

**Your Competitive Advantage**: You filter out low-value cases. Firms only get high-value cases. They'll pay premium pricing because they know every lead is worth their time.

**Clear Liability (80-100 points)**:
- Other driver admitted fault: +30 points
- Police report filed: +20 points
- Police cited other driver: +20 points
- Witnesses present: +15 points
- Video/dashcam evidence: +15 points

**Disputed Liability (40-79 points)**:
- Unclear who's at fault: 0 points
- No police report: -10 points
- Both drivers claim fault: -20 points
- No witnesses: -15 points

**No Liability (0-39 points)**:
- Claimant appears at fault: -30 points
- No evidence of other driver's fault: -20 points
- Claimant was speeding/reckless: -25 points

**Settlement Value Based on Liability**:
- Clear liability (80-100): Settlement likely 80-100% of damages
- Disputed liability (40-79): Settlement likely 40-60% of damages
- No liability (0-39): Settlement unlikely or very low

**Lead Value Based on Liability**:
- Clear liability: Lead worth $2,000 (firm confident they'll win)
- Disputed liability: Lead worth $800 (firm uncertain)
- No liability: Lead worth $100 (firm likely to reject)

### **The Trade Secret**

**Score every lead on liability strength.**

During qualification call, ask:
1. "Who was at fault?" (claimant's perspective)
2. "Was a police report filed?" (objective evidence)
3. "Did the police cite the other driver?" (legal determination)
4. "Were there witnesses?" (corroboration)
5. "Do you have dashcam or video?" (hard evidence)

Then:
1. Calculate liability score (0-100)
2. Price lead accordingly
3. Prioritize high-liability leads (sell first)
4. Hold low-liability leads (sell later or reject)

**Example Pricing Strategy**:

```
0-39           | $100       | Reject
40-59          | $600       | Low
60-79          | $1,200     | Medium
80-100         | $2,000+    | High
```

**Why Firms Pay More**: Clear liability = higher win rate = more predictable outcomes = more profitable.

**Your Competitive Advantage**: You filter out cases with disputed liability. Firms only get high-probability wins. They'll pay premium pricing.

**Why This Works**: You maximize conversion by matching lead volume to firm capacity.

The best way to acquire new firms is through testimonials from existing firms.

### **How It Works**

**Firm Testimonial Power**:
- Firm A: "CasePort sent us 50 leads. We converted 20 cases. Average settlement $350K. Best ROI we've seen."
- Firm B: "CasePort's leads are pre-qualified. We accept 70% (vs. 30% from other platforms). Highly recommend."
- Firm C: "CasePort's support team is responsive. They optimize our lead quality monthly. We're fully booked."

**Why It Works**: New firms trust other firms more than they trust you.

### **The Trade Secret**

**Collect firm testimonials obsessively.**

1. After firm's first 30 days, ask for testimonial
2. Ask: "How would you describe your experience with CasePort?"
3. Record video testimonial (more powerful than text)
4. Use testimonials in marketing materials
5. Feature top firms on your website

**Why This Works**: New firms see other firms are successful. They want to join.

**Your Competitive Advantage**: You have social proof. Competitors don't.
