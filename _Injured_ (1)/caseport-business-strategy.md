## **PART 1: THE EXCLUSIVE MODEL EXPLAINED**

### **How It Works**

**Traditional Model** (Non-Exclusive):
- Claimant gets matched with 3 firms
- All 3 firms compete for the case
- Claimant chooses 1 firm
- Other 2 firms lose the lead
- Firms pay $300-500/lead (because 2/3 lose)

**CasePort Exclusive Model**:
- Claimant gets matched with 1 firm (the exclusive partner)
- No competition
- Firm accepts 100% of qualified leads
- Firm pays $1,000-2,000/lead (because they get 100% of leads)
- You get 100% of revenue from that market

**Example: Los Angeles Auto Accident Market**
- Population: 4 million
- Annual car accidents: ~80,000
- Injury claims: ~40,000
- Qualified leads (medical records, injury): ~8,000/year
- Monthly leads: ~667

**Non-Exclusive Model**:
- 3 firms compete
- Each firm gets ~222 leads/month
- Firm pays $400/lead
- You get: $88,800/month revenue

**Exclusive Model**:
- 1 firm gets all 667 leads/month
- Firm pays $1,200/lead (premium for exclusivity)
- You get: $800,400/month revenue
- 9x more revenue per market

## **PART 2: WHY FIRMS PAY PREMIUM FOR EXCLUSIVE LEADS**

### **The Economics for Firms**

**Scenario: Mid-Size Auto Accident Firm**
- 5 attorneys
- Capacity: 50 active cases
- Average case value: $350K
- Average settlement: $350K
- Firm takes 33% contingency: $116K/case
- Annual revenue: 50 cases × $116K = $5.8M

**Non-Exclusive Model**:
- Gets 222 leads/month (shared with 2 other firms)
- Conversion rate: 12% (industry average)
- Cases per month: 222 × 12% = 26.6 cases/month
- But firm only has capacity for 50 cases total
- Firm is constantly overbooked, has to turn down cases
- Firm pays $400/lead = $88,800/month for leads they can't use

**Exclusive Model**:
- Gets 667 leads/month (all from CasePort)
- Conversion rate: 30% (because leads are pre-qualified + exclusive)
- Cases per month: 667 × 30% = 200 cases/month
- Firm has capacity for 50 cases, so they accept best 50/month
- Firm is fully booked, no wasted leads
- Firm pays $1,200/lead = $800,400/month
- But firm revenue: 50 cases × $116K = $5.8M/month
- ROI: $5.8M / $800K = 7.25x return

**Why Firm Prefers Exclusive**:
1. **No wasted leads** — All leads are pre-qualified, high conversion
2. **Full capacity utilization** — Firm is always at 100% capacity
3. **Better economics** — $1,200 per lead that converts is cheaper than $400 per lead that doesn't
4. **Predictable pipeline** — Firm knows exactly how many cases/month
5. **No competition** — Firm doesn't have to compete with 2 other firms for each lead

## **PART 3: HOW TO COMMAND PREMIUM PRICING ($1,200-2,000/LEAD)**

### **The Data Moat: Conversion Tracking**

**What You Track**:
1. **Lead Quality Score** (100-point scale)
   - Medical records: +20 points
   - Police report: +15 points
   - Witness statements: +15 points
   - Clear liability: +20 points
   - Injury severity: +15 points
   - Insurance policy limits: +15 points

2. **Claimant Profile**
   - Age, income, employment status
   - Medical history
   - Prior lawsuits
   - Social media presence (credibility)

3. **Case Viability**
   - Liability strength (clear vs. disputed)
   - Injury severity (minor vs. catastrophic)
   - Insurance coverage (adequate vs. insufficient)
   - Statute of limitations (time remaining)

4. **Conversion Prediction**
   - Probability of case acceptance: 0-100%
   - Estimated settlement range: $X-$Y
   - Estimated timeline: X months

**Example Lead Quality Scoring**:
```

- Conversion probability: 85%
- Estimated settlement: $300K-$500K
- Price: $1,800/lead

- Medical records: ✓ (+20)
- Police report: ✗ (0)
- Witness statements: ✗ (0)
- Clear liability: Disputed (+10)
- Injury severity: Minor (+5)
- Insurance limits: $50K (+5)
- Total: 40/100 points
- Conversion probability: 15%
- Estimated settlement: $10K-$30K
- Price: $300/lead
```

**Firm Value Proposition**:
> "Our leads convert at 30% (vs. 12% industry average) because we score every lead on 100+ data points. We only send you leads we predict will convert. You pay more per lead, but you waste fewer leads."

### **Why Firms Pay Premium**

**Math for Firm**:
- Industry average: $400/lead × 12% conversion = $33.33 per case
- CasePort exclusive: $1,200/lead × 30% conversion = $40/lead per case
- **CasePort is actually cheaper per converted case** (even though price is 3x higher)

**Plus**:
- Exclusive leads = no competition = firm accepts 100% of qualified leads
- Pre-qualified leads = firm doesn't waste attorney time on bad cases
- Predictable pipeline = firm can staff accordingly

## **PART 12: CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS**

### **What Must Go Right**

1. **Firm Acquisition** — You need to close 1 firm by Month 2. If you can't, the model fails.
   - Solution: Have warm introductions, not cold outreach
   - Solution: Start with smaller firms (5-10 attorneys), not mega-firms
   - Solution: Offer pilot program (lower risk for firm)

2. **Lead Quality** — Leads must convert at 30%+. If conversion rate is 12%, firms won't pay premium.
   - Solution: Strict qualification gate (medical records required)
   - Solution: Pre-funded wallet (firm only pays for accepted leads)
   - Solution: Conversion tracking (measure and optimize)

3. **Lead Volume** — You need 1,000+ leads/month by Month 6. If volume is too low, revenue won't scale.
   - Solution: Paid search (fastest way to get volume)
   - Solution: SEO/AEO (long-term organic growth)
   - Solution: Referral program (incentivize claimants to refer)

4. **Firm Retention** — Firms must stay with you long-term. If they churn, revenue drops.
   - Solution: Exclusive partnerships (firm can't switch to competitor)
   - Solution: Monthly check-ins (track satisfaction, address issues)
   - Solution: Continuous optimization (improve conversion rate, settlement rate)

5. **Bootstrapping** — You must be profitable by Month 6. If you run out of capital, the business fails.
   - Solution: Firm pre-funding (firms pay upfront)
   - Solution: Lean operations (minimal team, outsource where possible)
   - Solution: Reinvest all profit into growth

### **What Could Go Wrong**

1. **Firms Don't Want Exclusive** — Firms prefer non-exclusive (more leads to choose from)
   - Mitigation: Offer higher conversion rate guarantee (30% vs. 12%)
   - Mitigation: Offer lower price per lead ($1,200 vs. $2,000)
   - Mitigation: Offer pilot program to prove it works

2. **Lead Quality Drops** — As you scale, lead quality declines (more low-quality claimants)
   - Mitigation: Stricter qualification gate
   - Mitigation: Pre-funded wallet (firm only pays for accepted leads)
   - Mitigation: Conversion tracking (measure and optimize)

3. **Competition** — Other aggregators enter auto accident niche
   - Mitigation: Build data moat (conversion tracking, settlement predictions)
   - Mitigation: Lock in exclusive partnerships (firms can't switch)
   - Mitigation: Get to profitability fast (before competitors scale)

4. **Regulatory Issues** — ABA rules restrict lead generation practices
   - Mitigation: Legal review of all marketing
   - Mitigation: Ensure all claims are truthful
   - Mitigation: Transparent about business model

5. **Claimant Acquisition Cost Rises** — Paid search gets more expensive as you scale
   - Mitigation: Build organic search (SEO/AEO) to reduce CAC
   - Mitigation: Build referral program (lower CAC than paid)
   - Mitigation: Build brand (reduce CAC through brand awareness)
