# CasePort.io Claimant Landing Page - Strategic Framework

## Brand DNA (From Brand System Prompt)
- **Voice**: Dan Lok meets Apple — direct, authoritative, zero fluff, high-status. Never beg. Never oversell. State facts. Let the system speak for itself. Use "structured," "disciplined," "controlled."
- **Design Philosophy**: Engineered precision, not marketing fluff. Apple-level sophistication.
- **Color Palette**:
  - Background: #0A0E17 (deep navy-black)
  - Primary Text: #F1F3F5 (near-white)
  - Secondary Text: #B0B8C4 (muted gray)
  - Muted Text: #6B7280 (labels, footnotes)
  - Accent Cyan: #22D3EE (system elements, trust signals)
  - Gold: #F59E0B (ROI/value moments ONLY—never decorative)
  - System Green: #10B981 (active status, positive indicators)
  - Glass Border: rgba(255,255,255,0.06)
  - Glass Background: rgba(255,255,255,0.03)
- **Typography**:
  - Geist Sans: Body text, UI elements (weights: 400, 500, 600, 700)
  - Instrument Serif: Display headlines ONLY, used sparingly for "exhale" moments
  - JetBrains Mono: System labels, data points, specs, technical elements
- **Layout**: Asymmetric hero (60/40 split—copy left, system visualization right). Content max-width 1200px, centered. Sections flow in continuous gradient zones. No isolated floating cards in dark space.
- **Animations**: Subtle, purposeful. Never attention-seeking. Scroll-triggered fade-up (20px translateY, 0.6s ease-out). Staggered card reveals (0.1s delay). Breathing glow orbs on zone backgrounds (8-12s cycle). Floating hero card (gentle 10px vertical oscillation, 6s cycle). CTA button shimmer on hover (subtle light sweep). No bouncing, spinning, or parallax.

## PASTOR Conversion Sequence (Mandatory Structure)
1. **P — Problem**: Name the pain the claimant feels
2. **A — Amplify**: Show the cost of inaction
3. **S — Story/Solution**: Introduce CasePort as the system that solves it
4. **T — Testimony**: Social proof, metrics, third-party validation
5. **O — Offer**: What they get (system access, exclusivity, ROI)
6. **R — Response**: Clear CTA with urgency

## Inhale/Exhale Rhythm
- **Inhale Sections**: Bold statement, large type, minimal elements (e.g., spotlight quotes)
- **Exhale Sections**: Detailed proof, cards, specs, data (e.g., system specs grid)
- Alternate to create visual breathing

## Temperature Shifts (Color Intent)
- **Cyan**: System, trust, infrastructure
- **Gold**: Value, ROI, money (used sparingly—only ROI Calculator and value projections)
- **White**: Authority, clarity
- Shift cyan → gold → cyan across the page

## Trust Signals (Six Pillars)
1. **Market-Capped Exclusivity** — Limited firms per region to preserve lead quality
2. **Review-First Onboarding** — Every firm is evaluated before access is granted
3. **Transparent Reporting** — Full visibility into case flow activity and metrics
4. **Compliance-First Design** — Built with legal advertising compliance in mind
5. **Dedicated Support** — Direct line to the team, no ticket queues
6. **Qualification Standards** — Every opportunity is screened before routing

## ABA Compliance Rules (Critical)
- **No guaranteed outcomes**: Ever. Use "structured," "disciplined," "controlled" instead.
- **No misleading claims**: About conversion rates or case values.
- **Disclaimers on projections**: "Results vary based on market, practice area, and firm capacity" + "For illustrative purposes only"
- **No attorney advertising language**: Avoid claims that could be interpreted as attorney advertising.
- **Lead quality messaging**: Focus on "case acquisition system," "lead quality," "screening process"—not "guaranteed cases"

## Call vs. Form Strategic Decision

### CALL PRIORITY (Recommended for this page)
**Why calls win for claimants:**
- Claimants are injured, stressed, and need immediate reassurance
- Voice builds trust faster than forms (human connection)
- Urgency is higher (pain is acute)
- Conversion rate: 15-35% for calls vs. 5-12% for forms in personal injury
- Calls allow instant qualification and lead scoring
- Claimants feel "heard" immediately

**Call Strategy:**
- Primary CTA: "Call Now" (prominent, sticky header + hero section)
- Secondary CTA: "Schedule Callback" (for after-hours)
- Tertiary CTA: "Quick Form" (for those who prefer async)
- Phone number prominently displayed (with trust indicators: "Available 24/7", "Avg. response: 2 min")
- Click-to-call on mobile (automatic)
- Live chat as backup (for form-preferrers)

### Form as Secondary Path
- Quick intake form (3-5 fields max): Name, Phone, Injury Type, Brief Description
- Instant confirmation: "We'll call you within 2 minutes"
- No long forms (claimants are in pain, won't fill 20 fields)

## Lead Quality & Conversion Philosophy
- **Lead Scoring**: Claimants are pre-qualified by injury type, urgency, and location
- **No Lead Decay**: Every claimant is contacted immediately (call priority) or within 2 minutes (form)
- **Lead Recycling**: Claimants who don't convert on first call are re-engaged via email/SMS (with consent)
- **Qualification Standards**: Injury type, case value estimate, geographic fit
- **Target Conversion**: 25%+ lead-to-signed-case (vs. industry 2-25%)

## Conversion Optimization Rules
- **Mobile-first**: 60%+ of claimants use mobile
- **Fast load**: <2s load time (claimants are impatient)
- **One primary CTA**: "Call Now" (no competing CTAs above the fold)

- **Urgency**: "24/7 availability", "Avg. response: 2 min"

- **Friction-free**: 1-click call on mobile, 3-field form max
