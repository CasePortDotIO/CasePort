# Competitor Analysis: YouHaveALawyer.com (Saiontz & Kirk)

## BUSINESS MODEL EXTRACTION FOR CASEPORT

---

## 1. DIRECT ATTORNEY MODEL vs. CASEPORT'S MARKETPLACE MODEL

### YouHaveALawyer Approach (Traditional)
- **Direct firm ownership** — Saiontz & Kirk owns the platform and operates as a traditional law firm
- **Single brand** — All leads go to one firm (Saiontz & Kirk)
- **Geographic expansion** — Operates in MD, DC, VA, PA (multi-state but single brand)
- **Lead monetization** — Leads are internal; no external firm network

### CasePort Advantage (Marketplace Model)
- ✅ **Multi-firm network** — Top 3% vetted firms across all markets
- ✅ **Lead distribution** — Monetize leads by matching to best firm per case
- ✅ **Scalability** — Can expand to unlimited markets with firm partnerships
- ✅ **Revenue model** — Charge firms per qualified lead (higher margins than traditional law firm)

**Insight:** CasePort's marketplace model is MORE SCALABLE than YouHaveALawyer's single-firm model. You can expand nationally without hiring more lawyers.

---

## 2. LEAD QUALIFICATION STRATEGY

### YouHaveALawyer's Approach
- **3-step qualification form** (Step 1 of 3):
  1. "What type of accident were you involved in?" (Dropdown: Car, Motorcycle, MTA/Bus, Uber/Lyft)
  2. "What best describes your type of injury?" (Dropdown: Aches/Pains, Broken Bones, Cuts/Bruises, Concussion)
  3. "What type of treatment did you receive?" (Dropdown: No Treatment, Urgent Care, ER, Overnight Hospitalization, Shock Trauma)

- **Low friction** — Only 3 questions to get to form submission
- **Immediate CTA** — "Next ›" button after each question
- **No pre-qualification** — All leads accepted (volume play)

### CasePort's Current Approach
- **7-question qualification gate** (more rigorous)
- **Higher friction** — More questions = fewer leads but better quality
- **Pre-qualification** — Only top 1% of cases move forward

**Insight:** CasePort's 7-question gate is BETTER for quality but could be OPTIMIZED:
- Consider a 2-stage approach: 3-question quick qualifier → 4-question detailed qualifier
- First 3 questions should be identical to YouHaveALawyer's (accident type, injury type, treatment)
- This increases conversion rate while maintaining quality

## 8. MONETIZATION MODEL

### YouHaveALawyer's Model
- **Lead cost:** Unknown (internal firm, no external monetization)
- **Revenue:** Contingency fees from settlements (typically 33% of settlement)
- **Scalability:** Limited (single firm, geographic expansion only)
- **Margin:** High (contingency model, no upfront costs)

### CasePort's Model (Inferred)
- **Lead cost:** Charge firms per qualified lead (e.g., $50-200 per lead)
- **Revenue:** Lead fees + potential revenue share from settlements
- **Scalability:** High (multi-firm network, national expansion)
- **Margin:** Medium (lower than contingency, but higher volume)

**Insight:** CasePort's model is MORE SCALABLE but consider:
1. **Hybrid model** — Charge firms per lead + take small % of settlement (0.5-2%)
2. **Tiered pricing** — Charge more for high-value cases (medical malpractice > car accident)
3. **Performance-based pricing** — Charge based on case outcome (higher fee if settlement > $100K)
4. **Firm tier pricing** — Charge premium firms more, discount for new firms
